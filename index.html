⬢Hello World

Hello! My name is Enne. I am a data engineer living in Southern California.

I have the sweetest, dirtiest cat on the planet.



⬢Background & Education

I began my studies in architecture, switching my major a year and a half later to architectural engineering. I graduated from Jefferson University (then Philadelphia University) with a Bachelor of Science in Engineering, with a minor in foundational design, in 2014.


After college, I moved from my home state of Pennsylvania to Northern Virginia, working for a temp agency in Washington, DC and solving engineering homework on Fiverr while looking for engineering jobs. Within a month, the temp agency placed me at a trade association for a two-day assignment helping input data into an employee database. After the first day, I was asked to stay a week. After the third day, I was asked to stay the month. During my time temping at the association, I took the Fundamentals of Engineering exam and earned my EIT designation in early 2015. A few months later, I was hired on full-time as the database specialist, where I continued up the ranks to senior manager of data systems over the next few years.


Over the course of 7 years at the association, I honed my HTML skills learned in my teen years and developed working knowledge of SQL. I wanted to be more efficient in my job, so I enrolled in a data analytics bootcamp at University of California Irvine.



⬢Knowledge

My engineering education taught me to be a problem solver and my current job helped me realize a love for structured data. A data analytics bootcamp taught me how to be more efficient in programming, namely in ETL and data visualization. The bootcamp also instilled machine learning concepts as a new skill that I have been sharpening with online learning courses.
